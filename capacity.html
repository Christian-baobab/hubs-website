Capacity Building

The Hubs Capacity Building program focuses on building local capacity for program delivery and reiteration.

The Hubs implement Capacity Building at sites where such informal or parallel education systems are in operation. In these contexts, Refugees and Refugee-Led Organizations are engaged in providing education either by local NGO's or as incentive-based teachers working within the UNHCR or local RLO's.

What We Do


By Capacity Building, the OSUN Hubs offer refugees up-skilling in four areas:

1. Full training program for refugee facilitators.

2. Mentorship training for teachers of refugees to ensure high quality education that can lead to RhEAP readiness.

3. Administrative capacity-building for refugee-led organizations (RLO's) hosting Hubs’ programs.

4. Researcher training for refugees researching Hubs and Working Group-related work.

All four of these components are critical to the Hubs’ model; since 2020, we have prioritized implementing programming in collaboration with refugee-led organizations.
