Acces to higher education

Realizing Higher Education Access Program (RhEAP)

Founded by Bard College in 2020, RhEAP builds on Bard’s long-standing commitment—dating back to the 1950s—to expanding access to higher education for displaced and crisis-affected youth. RhEAP is a 10–12 month university bridging and preparation program that equips students aged 16–24 with essential, transferable skills for a rapidly evolving world. The curriculum emphasizes critical thinking, academic and professional English, analytical reasoning, and effective communication—while also building subject knowledge in the Humanities, Social Sciences, and STEM. RhEAP is global in scope, with programs operating across Eastern Africa, the MENA region, South Asia, and Southeast Asia. Through virtual classrooms, students from diverse backgrounds collaborate across borders, enriching learning through dialogue and group work. Courses are grounded in research-based, trauma-informed, and student-centered pedagogy, with local contextualization at every site. The program uses a blended learning model: Bard professors teach online, supported by in-person teaching assistants trained in Bard’s educational methods. Admissions is holistic and includes academic records, essay-based applications, interviews, and assessments in English and critical thinking. Students below a B1 English level are enrolled in preparatory English courses prior to joining the main program.

Course Structre: Four Modules

RhEAP
Module 1: Study Skills, Digital Literacy, Foundations of Academic Writing, History of the World

Module 2: Advanced Academic Writing, Science Literacy

Module 3: Bard Network university courses alongside matriculated peers. OSUN Network Collaborative course

Module 4: Higher Education and Career Pathways Preparation


Credits and Applying
 All students who complete RhEAP receive up to 20 transferable Bard College credits, which can be applied toward future studies or employment applications.

Language, Images, and Critical Thinking Program (LIT)

LIT developed out of a 50+ year old program at Bard College, which emphasizes writing as a tool for thinking and learning. Adapted to serve youth between the ages of 14 and 24 in displacement and crisis contexts, LIT utilizes innovative pedagogical approaches to teach English language to non-native speakers, critical thinking, analysis, and communication skills. English language development, as well as critical thinking, analysis, digital literacy, and communication skills. Low-cost and scalable, the program is highly adaptable and is conducted by refugee or local facilitators trained in a pedagogy that can operate with or without technological support. LIT has supported recently displaced students in locations where formal schooling is not yet in place; it has also operated in more protracted crisis areas where learning is impoverished.
